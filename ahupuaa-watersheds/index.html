Ahupuaʻa Watershed Mapper

Ahupuaʻa are traditional Hawaiian land divisions that run mauka to makai — mountain ridge to reef — so each was meant to hold a whole watershed. This map lays the old boundaries over stream channels and basins computed from a USGS 10 m elevation model, so you can see where the divisions and the actual drainage agree, and where they part.

Hover the map to inspect an ahupuaʻa. Click to pin it and see its computed watershed.

Boundaries: State of Hawaiʻi Statewide GIS Program (OHA / DLNR). Elevation: USGS 3DEP 1/3″ (~10 m). Flow routing: D8 on a depression-filled DEM.

Ahupuaʻa boundaries are cultural and legal, not purely hydrologic — where they diverge from the computed drainage is the interesting part, not an error in either.
