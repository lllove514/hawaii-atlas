Hawaiʻi Atlas

Maps of the islands, built from public data.

I have family in Hawaiʻi, and a trip to visit them turned into this project. The islands are full of data hiding in plain sight: warm water sitting over the reefs, fresh black rock on the Big Island’s flanks, ahupuaʻa names on road signs marking land divisions far older than any modern survey. Back home, I started pulling the public records behind what I’d been standing on — NOAA satellites, USGS geology and elevation, the state GIS — and building the maps I wished I’d had while I was there.

Every map is hand-built: vanilla JavaScript drawing on a canvas — no map library, no framework, no build step — with a small, documented Python pipeline turning each raw dataset into the compact files the browser loads. The whole site is static.
